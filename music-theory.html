2. Swaras: Types & Notation Symbols

Swaras are classified as:

  • Shuddha (Natural): The default pitch of the note (e.g., Re, Ga, Dha).
  • Komal (Flat): Slightly lower in pitch than the natural note. Re, Ga, Dha, and Ni can be Komal.
  • Teevra (Sharp): Only Ma can be Teevra (Ma becomes sharper than its natural pitch).

Notation symbols used in Sargam:

4. Ragas ‚Äì The Soul of Indian Classical Music

A Raga is a melodic framework. It defines a mood, a time of day, and an emotion. Each raga is a combination of specific notes (swaras) and prescribed rules of ascent (Aroha) and descent (Avaroha).

Popular Ragas Overview:

  • Raga Yaman: Uses Teevra Ma; performed in the evening. Romantic and serene in mood.
  • Raga Bhairav: Komal Re and Komal Dha; early morning raga, evokes peace and seriousness.
  • Raga Bhopali: Uses only 5 notes (S R G P D); bright and uplifting. No Ma or Ni.
  • Raga Des: Joyful and expressive. Often used in monsoon-themed compositions.
  • Raga Darbari Kanada: Night raga. Deep and heavy, used in serious and majestic themes.
